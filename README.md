Summary of findigs:
After evaluating four machine learning models—K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and Support Vector Machines (SVM)—the results showed that accuracy alone was not a reliable performance measure.
Although all models achieved high accuracy scores (around 88–90%), the Recall and F1-scores were relatively low, indicating the presence of an imbalanced dataset.
Among the models, the Decision Tree performed best in identifying positive cases, achieving the highest Recall and F1-score. 
Logistic Regression produced the highest ROC-AUC score, demonstrating better class discrimination ability. In contrast, the SVM required significantly longer training time without improving overall performance.
The variable importance analysis also showed that macroeconomic factors, particularly Euribor interest rates, had the greatest influence on subscription probability. 
In addition, communication-related variables, such as previous customer interactions, strongly affected customer responses. 
Overall, the results indicate that economic conditions and campaign management factors are more important predictors of term deposit subscriptions than demographic characteristics.
